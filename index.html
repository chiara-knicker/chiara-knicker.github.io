About Me

Who am I?

Hello there! I'm Chiara, a final year computer science student at The University of Edinburgh. Originally from Germany, my international background adds a diverse perspective to my journey.


What interests me?

During my year abroad last year at UC Santa Cruz, I discovered my interest in cybersecurity and participated in various competitions like the NSA Codebreaker, CyberForce and MITRE eCTF. These competitions improved my problem-solving skills and reinforced my eagerness to learn and explore new challenges. I joined the cybersecurity club and also contributed to the creation of the women in cybersecurity club. When I returned from my year abroad, I joined the University of Edinburgh cybersecurity club to keep learning and sharpen my skills.

Apart from cybersecurity, I am interested in computer networks and embedded systems. My experiences as a tutor for an embedded systems class and grader for a computer networks class taught me how to learn quickly and take the initiative to delve deeper into my interests.


What do I do outside of academics?

Beyond academics, I am an active member of the university's gymnastics club. There, I've showcased my skills in various competitions, nurturing discipline and teamwork.


What's next?

In addition to my specific interests, I am excited to explore new areas within computer science, as I believe that each challenge presents valuable learning opportunities. As I seek internships, my goal is to contribute to projects, expand my skillset, and make the most of every experience presented to me.
